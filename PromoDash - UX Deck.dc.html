PART ONE

The Platform

The problem, the market gap, the buyer, and the architecture that collapses the anxiety peak.

THE PROBLEM

Promo buying is a stress purchase, not an inspirational one.

It's triggered by an obligation, a new hire, an event, a deadline, a budget that must be spent. The buyer is time-poor, risk-averse, and personally accountable if it goes wrong.

They are optimising for safety, not creativity.

Every existing product forces a trade-off between scale and simplicity — giant catalogs behind reps and quotes, or clean interfaces gated by subscriptions, minimums, and 3–4 week production.

TIME-POOR
Wants to be done in minutes, not days.
RISK-AVERSE
Needs proof before committing, not after.
ACCOUNTABLE
Personally blamed if it's late or looks cheap.
THE GOAL

The design goal is singular.

Get an anxious buyer from landing to a placed, proofed order in minutes, by delivering proof before the decision (mockup, price, ship-by date) rather than after it.

THE ONE RULE
If it slows the decision, it doesn't belong.
Fewer choices. Confident defaults. Proof up front. No dead ends into "contact a rep."

MARKET GAP

Five UX failures we design against

4imprint
Pricing and artwork run through reps and an email proofing loop; a 30k-SKU catalog maximises choice anxiety.
→ Make the proofed, priced order instant and self-serve.
SwagUp
Real value sits behind subscription tiers; standard production is 3–4 weeks; MOQ ~25 plus storage fees.
→ No membership wall, sub-week shipping, all-in pricing.
Swag.com
Closest on interface, but no true no-minimum path — teams get pushed into bulk and warehousing.
→ Match the clean UX, beat them on order-what-you-need.
Printfection
Inventory-led, so the reorder may be out of stock; oriented to swag admins, not first-time buyers.
→ Production-backed hero SKUs; availability before commit.
PrintStop
MOQ-of-one, but a broad, procurement-flavoured print-shop experience across 500+ products.
→ Keep MOQ-of-one; re-skin around decision speed.

DECORATION

Four methods that matter — the buyer names none

They pick an outcome; we map it to the right method and prove it in 3D.

Laser engraving
TONE-ON-TONE
Premium, permanent mark on stainless / powder-coat metal.
DURABILITY · HIGHEST
Best for Stanley/YETI, 1-color logos, luxury gifts.
UV printing
FULL COLOR
Full-color logos, gradients, photos on metal / glass / plastic.
DURABILITY · HIGH
Multi-color logos, small runs, vibrant campaigns.
Screen printing
1–6 SPOT
High-volume single / limited-color on cylindrical items.
COST · CHEAP AT SCALE
500+ runs, ceramic mugs, pint glasses.
Sublimation
FULL WRAP
Full wraparound full-color on coated substrates.
LOW-MOQ FRIENDLY
Coated aluminum bottles, mugs, all-over designs.
